Hi! My name is Rodrigo Carvalho,

 a multidisciplinary artist, programmer, and Mechanical Engineering student. I have knowledge in various tools and software, including Ableton Live, Touch Designer, Resolume, PyCharm, Blender, Unreal, and Visual Studio. I have experience in programming, interactive installations/event lighting, music production, and have worked with renowned brands such as Heineken, Nike, Artwalk, Rayban, and Ingresse. Uma publicação compartilhada por THE FORCE (@theforcex)

HEINEKEN x ROCK IN RIO x THE FORCE
At Rock in Rio, I worked with The Force to develop two interactive installations for Heineken as part of their Green Your City campaign. Both installations incorporated spaces that harmonized nature and technology, promoting direct interaction with the audience and providing a unique experience for each person. In the first booth, 22 LIDAR sensors were used to modulate lasers and sounds, creating a reactive digital forest. In the Biosphere, there were several pots with plants and moss that responded to touch energy and transformed it into beats performed by indigenous rapper Brisa Flow.

Uma publicação compartilhada por Unsee Space (@unsee.space)

NIKE x ARTWALK x UNSEE SPACE
In an immersive and visually comfortable environment, the user could blend selected music loops created by artists using a MIDI controller, creating their own beat in real-time. Through a user-friendly interface, the user could use a touch screen to record a 30-second video that switched between 2 cameras. This video was then made available at the end of the experience via a QR code, directly in Instagram format, and with Nike and Artwalk masks.
OTHER PROJECTS
In the panel (generated with a cross-browser JavaScript library), I included snippets from some of the artistic installation and live marketing projects I participated in. Among them was the installation created for 'Rayban Ocupa', in which we used Arduino, ultrasonic sensors, and a NASA API to control the movement of 6 moving lights. I also took part in the 'Célula' installation, created by Arthur Boeira for the Canva festival, which featured a projection of a particle system simulating a colony of bacteria. The visuals and soundtrack were modulated by the movement of people in the room, using a Kinect sensor.
Additionally, I contributed to mapping and lighting operation for Cartel011 and Ingresse events. During Design Week, together with Erotides and Arthur Boeira, we developed an installation with mapped projection and scenography, exploring the harmony between nature and technology under the SolarPunk perspective. I also contributed to photogrammetry and 3D modeling of sneakers for Mundo Sneaker.
I would like to highlight the mapped projection project carried out for Alexandre Vianna's artwork 'Cura', exhibited at Pixel Show.
VIEWING ROOM
In this web experience, you can dive into a 3D space developed by me, walking around and viewing the "Fluid" collection, featuring some of the artworks I have created recently. It's truly fascinating to freely roam in a completely digital new world, as if you could sense each artwork, explore various unexpected perspectives and angles, immersing yourself in this unique atmosphere. This technique opens up countless possibilities. Please note that the viewing room is not optimized for mobile devices yet (it will be very soon).

About me
My name is Rodrigo Carvalho, I'm 25 years old, and I live in the capital city of São Paulo. From a very young age, I have been fascinated by art, technology, and physics (yes, physics). I have been privileged to have access to tools that have allowed me to develop in areas that align with my interests. I would say that an important point of convergence in my story happened when I was 17 years old and discovered Ableton Live, which brought together the worlds of art and technology. Since then, I have never ceased to be amazed by the possibilities that this fusion can offer us.
